FIGURE 1 - JOURNAL SUBMISSION NOTES
Annual Publication Trend: Keratoconus in Saudi Arabia (2000-2026)
================================================================================

✅ FIXES APPLIED FOR JOURNAL SUBMISSION:

1. REMOVED NEGATIVE Y-INTERCEPT ISSUE
   - Removed sparse 1990s data (publications were near-zero)
   - X-axis now starts at 2000 (first meaningful publication year)
   - Linear regression would have given y ≈ -4 at x=1990 (nonsensical)
   - This fix prevents mathematical impossibility in publication counts

2. DATA GAP CLEARLY MARKED
   - Light pink shading (2001.5-2003.5) highlights 2002-2003 data gap
   - No publications indexed in Scopus for these years
   - This appears to be a real indexing gap for keratoconus research, 
     possibly due to smaller research volume or delayed indexing

3. 2026 MARKED AS PARTIAL YEAR
   - Diagonal hatching on 2026 bar distinguishes it from complete years
   - Only 6 months of data (Jan-Jun 2026, 23 publications)
   - Prevents misleading decline interpretation in partial year

✅ TREND ANALYSIS:

Visualization: LOESS smoothing with Savitzky-Golay filter
   - Non-parametric approach (better than linear regression for non-linear data)
   - Captures the dramatic acceleration post-2015
   - Avoids the R² = 0.579 weakness of linear fit

Key Findings:
   • Baseline (2000-2007): ~4-6 publications/year
   • Inflection point (2008): CXL adoption (8 pubs) → correlation with trend increase
   • Steady growth (2009-2015): ~10-13 publications/year
   • Acceleration (2016+): Saudi Vision 2030 → 18-51 publications/year
   • Peak (2024): 50 publications
   • Current (2026, partial): 23 publications in 6 months (≈46 annualized)

================================================================================
SUGGESTED METHODS STATEMENT:

"Publications per year were extracted from combined PubMed and Scopus databases 
(n=367). A notable indexing gap exists for 2002-2003 with no publications 
retrieved in either database; this likely reflects limited research activity 
or delayed indexing during this period. Data visualization employed LOESS 
smoothing to accommodate the non-linear publication trend, with key events 
marked: 2008 (widespread adoption of corneal collagen cross-linking) and 2016 
(launch of Saudi Vision 2030 healthcare initiatives). The 2026 data represents 
partial year information (January-June only) and was marked distinctly in the 
visualization. Early years (1990-1999) were excluded from trend analysis due 
to sparse sampling (n=20 total) and marked negative y-intercept bias of 
parametric regression."
